A credit score checker is a valuable tool for individuals to monitor their creditworthiness through a three-digit numerical representation. What is a credit score? How is a credit score determined?

A credit score, ranging from 300 to 900, statistically represents a person's creditworthiness, indicating their ability to repay borrowed sums. A higher score is advantageous for loan or credit card applications, offering better terms and interest rates, while a lower score suggests a history of late payments and financial irresponsibility

Credit bureaus gather following data to determine a credit score:

Personal information
Credit history length
New credit
Amount owed
Payment history
Credit mix
Why is it necessary to have a high credit score?

Maintaining a high credit score is essential to increase your loan eligibility, faster loan approvals, benefits of lower interest rates, access to credit cards with alluring rewards, and higher loan amounts qualification and credit card limits.

  1. Increases Loan Eligibility and Accessibility: A high credit score signifies responsible borrowing, enhancing the chances of swift pre-approved loans without processing delays
  2. Low-Interest Rates: A high credit score leads to lower loan interest rates, saving you money in the long run
What aspects are considered when determining credit score?

The Credit Bureaus take into account the following elements when calculating credit scores:

  1. Credit history and it’s length: History includes information about your account number, credit usage, and any missed or late payments and a long history of on-time payments leads up to 15% of your credit score
  2. Credit score inquiries: This includes loan type, requested amount, and individual or joint application details
How can one improve their credit score?

  • Pay your loan instalments on time

  • Pay your bills on time and avoid exceeding your credit limit

  • Pay off any outstanding debts or bills

  • Avoid submitting multiple credit card or loan applications

Few other factors to look for

000 and STD Codes

Credit bureaus use "000" for accounts in good standing, paid on time. "STD" stands for "Standard," indicating timely payments or dues within 90 days

Dues in Credit Report

Get your credit report from CIBIL, look for any non-"000" or "STD" statuses, dispute errors, and monitor payments for timeliness

Change of Address Impact

Frequent address changes don't impact credit scores but can raise red flags for lenders, affecting credit approval

Unauthorised Report Access

If a lender checks your credit without consent, dispute with the bureau, complain to the lender, and monitor your report to protect your score

Derogatory Remarks

Derogatory remarks in a CIBIL report, like late payments or defaults, can impact credit score and make obtaining credit challenging.

Unaware about loan inquiries in the report?

If you see loans without your knowledge in your credit bureau report, you should first contact the credit bureau and the concerned lender to raise a dispute and request them to investigate the matter. They are required to investigate and respond to your dispute within a specified time frame. 

If you are not satisfied with their response or resolution, you may approach the Banking Ombudsman appointed by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). The Banking Ombudsman is an independent and impartial authority that addresses complaints and grievances related to banking services, including credit reporting issues. 

You can file a complaint with the Banking Ombudsman online or in writing, providing all the necessary details and supporting documents. The Banking Ombudsman will then investigate the matter and provide a resolution based on their findings.
